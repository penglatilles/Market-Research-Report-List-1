<p><p>The oncology market features various drug types used to treat cancer. Antimetabolites disrupt cell division by mimicking natural substances, while alkaloids inhibit cell growth by interfering with the mitotic process. Hormone therapies target hormone-dependent tumors, blocking or mimicking hormones. Targeted drugs focus on specific molecules involved in cancer progression, enhancing efficacy while minimizing damage to healthy cells. Platinum-based drugs, like cisplatin, cause DNA damage, leading to cell death. Other drugs include immunotherapies and monoclonal antibodies, expanding treatment options.</p></p>
