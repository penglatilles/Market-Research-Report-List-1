<p><p>The ceramic cup market is categorized into three temperature ranges based on firing processes. Low-fire ceramics (700–900°C) offer vibrant colors and styles but have lower durability. Medium-fire ceramics (1000–1200°C) balance aesthetics and strength, making them popular for functional dinnerware. High-fire ceramics (≥1200°C) provide superior durability and heat resistance, ideal for professional use and long-lasting items. Each type serves distinct consumer needs, from artistic pieces to everyday functional products, influencing market demand and trends.</p></p>
