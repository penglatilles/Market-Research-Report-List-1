<p><p>The semiconductor market is categorized into three main precursor types: Si precursors, metal precursors, and high-K precursors. Si precursors, primarily silanes, are essential for silicon-based wafer fabrication, enabling the deposition of thin films. Metal precursors, often used in atomic layer deposition, facilitate the creation of metallic layers and compounds necessary for electronic components. High-K precursors, used in dielectric applications, enhance transistor performance by enabling the fabrication of thin, insulating layers with high dielectric constants in advanced semiconductor devices.</p></p>
