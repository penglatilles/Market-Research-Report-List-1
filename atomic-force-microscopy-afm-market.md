<p><p>Atomic Force Microscopy (AFM) is a high-resolution imaging technique that involves scanning a nanoscale tip over a sample's surface to measure forces between the tip and the sample. The market encompasses various types, including Contact AFM, which provides high-resolution imaging by keeping the tip in constant contact; Non-contact AFM, which minimizes tip-sample interaction; Dynamic Contact AFM, utilizing oscillation to enhance imaging fidelity; and Tapping AFM, which intermittently contacts the surface for delicate imaging of soft materials, catering to diverse research needs.</p></p>
