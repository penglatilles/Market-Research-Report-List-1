<p><p>Lactase is an enzyme that breaks down lactose, a sugar found in milk and dairy products, into simpler sugars like glucose and galactose. It is essential for individuals with lactose intolerance, as it aids in the digestion of lactose, preventing symptoms like bloating, gas, and diarrhea. Lactase is widely used in the food and beverage industry to produce lactose-free products, dietary supplements, and pharmaceuticals.</p><p>The Lactase Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 5.1% during the forecast period. This growth is driven by increasing awareness of lactose intolerance, rising demand for lactose-free dairy products, and advancements in enzyme technology. The growing preference for functional foods and dietary supplements also contributes to market expansion. Geographically, North America and Europe dominate the market due to high consumer awareness and demand for lactose-free products, while the Asia-Pacific region is witnessing rapid growth due to increasing lactose intolerance cases and rising disposable incomes.</p><p>Key trends in the lactase market include the development of heat-stable lactase enzymes for use in high-temperature processing, the introduction of innovative lactose-free product lines, and the expansion of plant-based lactase sources. These factors are expected to drive the market's growth in the coming years.</p></p>

<p><p>The lactase market is segmented into Neutral Lactase and Acid Lactase based on enzyme activity and application. Neutral Lactase operates optimally at a neutral pH (around 6-7) and is primarily used in dairy products like milk to break down lactose, enhancing digestibility. Acid Lactase functions effectively in acidic environments (pH 3-5) and is commonly used in fermented dairy products like yogurt. Both types cater to lactose-intolerant consumers, improving product accessibility and expanding market reach for dairy-based foods.</p></p>
