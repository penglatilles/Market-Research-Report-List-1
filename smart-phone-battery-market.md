<p><p>The smartphone battery market primarily features two types: lithium-ion (Li-ion) and nickel-metal hydride (NiMH) batteries. Li-ion batteries dominate due to their high energy density, lightweight, and longer lifespan, making them ideal for modern smartphones. In contrast, NiMH batteries are heavier and have lower energy density, but they are more environmentally friendly and less prone to overheating. While Li-ion batteries are preferred for their performance, NiMH is still used in some niche applications due to its sustainability.</p></p>
