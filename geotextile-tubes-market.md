<p><p>Geotextile tubes are categorized into woven and nonwoven types, each serving distinct purposes in environmental and civil engineering applications. Woven geotextiles are constructed from interlaced fibers, providing strength and durability for erosion control and soil stabilization. In contrast, nonwoven geotextiles are made from randomly arranged fibers, offering excellent filtration, drainage, and geotechnical properties. Both types are crucial for managing sediment, protecting shorelines, and enhancing infrastructure, catering to diverse needs in the growing geotextile tube market.</p></p>
