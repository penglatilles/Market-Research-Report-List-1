<p><p>Refuse-Derived Fuel (RDF) is produced from various types of municipal solid waste, processed to generate energy. The market for RDF is categorized into two main types: Dense RDF and Loose RDF. Dense RDF features compacted, pelletized materials, making it more suitable for industrial applications and efficient transportation. In contrast, Loose RDF consists of irregularly shaped, shredded waste with a bulkier form, often used in local energy production facilities. Both types serve as alternative fuels to reduce landfill usage and promote sustainable energy sources.</p></p>
