<p><p>The ADHD therapeutics market is primarily divided into two categories: stimulants and non-stimulants. Stimulants, such as methylphenidate and amphetamines, are the most commonly prescribed medications, known for their effectiveness in increasing attention and reducing impulsivity by enhancing dopamine levels in the brain. Non-stimulants, like atomoxetine and guanfacine, provide alternatives for those who may not respond well to stimulants or experience undesirable side effects. Both types of medications are vital in managing ADHD symptoms and improving patient quality of life.</p></p>
