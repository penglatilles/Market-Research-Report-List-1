<p><p>The nuclear connector market includes two primary types: organic materials and glass-metal connectors. Organic materials, such as polymers, offer lightweight and flexible solutions suitable for various nuclear applications, providing excellent insulation and resistance to radiation. In contrast, glass-metal connectors combine the durability of metal with the sealing properties of glass, ensuring robust performance in extreme environments. Both types are crucial for maintaining reliable electrical connections in nuclear systems, enhancing safety and efficiency in energy generation and management.</p></p>
